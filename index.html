Paradise Graves Presents
Graves
The Card Game of Ranthal · Complete Rules · 2–6 Players

A game of guile, memory, and measured risk. Bury your cards, wager your coin, and outwit your rivals.

Overview

Each player builds a hand of four suits representing the forces of nature and fortune, then buries 3 cards face-down as their Grave. When all Graves are set, every player reveals simultaneously. Highest score claims the pot. Most coin when the deck runs dry wins.

The Deck

52 cards across four suits, each numbered 1–13. One special card — The Raven — acts as a wild.

🌿
Root
Pairs & sequences score highest. Storm breaks sequences.
🔥
Ember
High face values. Snuffed to zero by Tide.
🌊
Tide
Negates all Ember cards present. Low values.
⚡
Storm
Pair = d6 bonus. Triple = score ×2. Breaks Root sequences.
The Raven: Declare any suit and value 1–10 before burying. Value locks on burial. Worth 3 coin at round end if held unburied.
Setup
  • One player hosts and shares the 4-letter room code in Discord. Others join with that code.
  • The host deals — 5 cards per player from the shared deck. No one sees another's hand.
  • Each player starts with 20 coin. Ante of 2 coin enters the pot each round.
A Round of Graves
Phase I
Draw
Each player draws 2 cards from the shared deck.
Phase II
Burial
Select 3 cards to place face-down as your Grave. Simultaneous — no one sees yours.
Phase III
Wager
Raise, call, or fold. Folding costs your ante but saves you from the pot.
Phase IV
Reveal
All players reveal at once. Scores are calculated automatically.

Graves — Advanced Rules
Advanced Play
The Haunt

Once per game, before burying your Grave, secretly peek at one card in another player's already-buried Grave. No cards may move. Mark it used.

Storm Cards
  • Storm Pair adds a d6 roll to your score.
  • Storm Triple Crown doubles the entire score.
  • Any Storm card breaks Root sequences.
Folding & Survival

Folding costs your ante but saves you from pot loss. At 0 coin, Rise from the Grave once — take 5 coin from the pot to re-enter.

End of Game

When the deck is exhausted, play one final round with hand cards only. Most coin wins. Ties resolved by The Last Rite — each tied player buries one card; highest value wins.
